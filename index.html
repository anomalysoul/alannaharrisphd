About

Scholar.
Practitioner.
Anomaly.

I am an equity-centered educator and qualitative researcher with over a decade of experience spanning K–12 classrooms, higher education, federal grant work, and national conference stages. My Ph.D. in Urban Education from UW-Milwaukee grounds my practice in culturally responsive literacy, healing-centered pedagogy, and critical mindfulness.

I believe learning environments must affirm the full humanity of every student — their language, their culture, their body, their joy. My work sits at the intersection of Black feminist epistemology, literacy justice, and transformative teacher development.

When I'm not in a classroom or writing a research paper, I'm behind the decks — because DJing and teaching are the same practice: reading the room, holding the energy, and knowing when to drop the beat.

Anomaly Soul

Signature Programs

Anomaly Soul is the vessel for transformative learning outside the institution — where scholarship meets spirit, and education becomes a practice of liberation.

These offerings are available as standalone workshops, multi-session intensives, or as part of school and community PD programming. Each one is designed to shift — not just inform.

01
Pedagogy of the Whole Self
A healing-centered professional development experience for educators ready to teach from their full humanity — body, spirit, history, and practice integrated into one.
02
DJ as Designer
A framework for teaching synthesis, reading the room, and composing meaning — using DJing as a methodology for creative and academic literacy.
03
The Clout Detox
A critical inquiry into performative pedagogy and ragebait education culture — helping teachers reclaim their practice from the metrics of virality.
04
Write to Heal / Write to Lead
Writing as a technology of transformation — for students and educators who are ready to use the page as a site of healing, resistance, and power.
05
Curriculum as Cultural Work
A deep-dive into curriculum design grounded in Black feminist epistemology, culturally sustaining pedagogy, and justice-centered learning outcomes.
06
CliftonStrengths for Educators
Strengths-based coaching and team development for educators — discovering and deploying your natural genius in service of your students and community.
